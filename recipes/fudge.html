Old-Fashioned Chocolate Fudge


Home

Description

These tasty grilled pork chops are flavored with an easy homemade seasoning rub for a spicy, smoky flavor. They're quick to prep and always a favorite at BBQ parties.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups white sugar
  • ½ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, sliced and softened
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. Grease an 8-inch square baking pan.
  3. Place sugar and cocoa powder in a medium saucepan; stir in milk until blended. Heat over medium-high, stirring constantly, until mixture comes to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat to low; attach a candy thermometer to the pan. Simmer, without stirring, until mixture reaches 238 degrees F (114 degrees C), about 10 minutes.
  5. If you don't have a candy thermometer, drop a small amount of the mixture into cold water; if it forms a soft ball that flattens when removed from the water and placed on a flat surface, it's ready.
  6. Remove from the heat; set aside to cool to 110 degrees F (43 degrees C), 50 to 70 minutes. Do not disturb fudge as it's cooling.
  7. Add butter and vanilla to the cooled fudge. Beat with a wooden spoon until well incorporated and fudge loses its sheen; do not under beat.
  8. Pour fudge into the prepared pan; press to flatten. Cool completely at room temperature or in the refrigerator before cutting into 1-inch squares.
  9. Enjoy!
